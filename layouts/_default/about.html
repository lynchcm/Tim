My approach to painting has always been based on observations made whilst walking in the Derbyshire landscape that surrounds me.

I have always been drawn to woodlands and other wild places as a starting point for these works and it is with these interpretations whilst out exploring that I bring back to my studio, these may be drawings, quick sketches, photographs or just memories.

In order to be able to capture some of those fleeting glimpses within the landscape I will often return to the same places for months sometimes years in order to observe the seasonal changes in these visual landscapes and to contemplate the subtle changes that occur. I will then attempt to reinterpret and play around with these images in order to truly reflect those experiences.

The paintings are built up using a process of thin washes to establish form and space and then worked over with oils, varnish and in some cases pastels to add to the sense of depth.
